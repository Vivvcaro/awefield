Clinical measurement

Outcomes you can
document and defend

Resilience Scale

Trait change in coping mechanisms

The resilience scale is administered at intake, mid-programme, and close. Delta scores provide a peer-reviewed outcome metric suitable for clinical records, insurance submissions, and research contribution.

Connor & Davidson, 2003 · Validated in 40+ languages

HRV

Heart Rate Variability Monitoring

Optional integration with consumer wearables (Apple Watch, Garmin, Polar). Reads autonomic state in real time and adapts integration questioning depth — containment or depth, based on patient's live state.

Thayer et al., 2012 · Neuroscience & Biobehavioural Reviews

NLP

Language Pattern Analysis

Every word spoken during integration is analysed for semantic proximity to the stated therapeutic intention. The system tracks linguistic evolution — from avoidance to embodiment, from past to present.

Pennebaker et al., LIWC framework · Adapted for therapeutic use

The clinical protocol

End-to-end structured pathway —
intake through to programme report

Step 01

Patient baseline assessment

Validated intake using a resilience scale, prior altered-state experience scoring across five domains, and trait-level psychological profiling. Generates a clinical readiness score and recommended entry pathway.

Validated resilience scale + ASC scoring
Output: readiness tier + recommended protocol path
Step 02

Intention setting & plan generation

The client selects from five clinical intention pathways, or co-creates a specific therapeutic focus with the therapist. The system generates a personalised programme.

Pathways: Deep Rest · Expanded Awareness · Creative Flow · Emotional Processing · Clarity
Step 03

Session — induction to close

Mood assessment, somatic activation, a personalised music journey with optional voice recording, and reflective prompts. HRV monitoring via wearable if enabled.

Pre-session mood + personalised journey + HRV data
Step 04

AI-guided integration — 5 dimensions

Post-session integration across somatic, visual, auditory, relational, and memory dimensions — cross-referencing in-session voice recordings.

Session fully recorded — accessible to patient and clinician
Step 05 · Programme Close

Clinical report — outcomes & recommendations

At programme close, Awe Field produces a comprehensive clinical report: HRV trajectory, peak moment mapping, language pattern analysis, resilience scale delta, and continuation pathway.

Evidence base

The science behind
Awe Field

Awe Field is grounded in peer-reviewed research across music therapy, altered states neuroscience, HRV biofeedback, and consciousness studies.

Key research areas

Music & Altered States

How music induces
non-ordinary states

Research by Kaelen et al. (2015, 2018) demonstrates that music is a primary driver of the subjective experience in psychedelic therapy — including emotional breakthrough, imagery, and oceanic boundlessness. Music-evoked altered states share neural signatures with pharmacologically induced ones.

Kaelen 2015Kaelen 2018Default Mode Network
HRV & Autonomic State

Heart Rate Variability
as a clinical window

Thayer et al. (2012) established HRV as a validated proxy for autonomic nervous system flexibility — directly linked to emotional regulation, cognitive flexibility, and therapeutic readiness. Real-time HRV enables adaptive modulation of session depth and integration pacing.

Thayer 2012Autonomic NSNeurovisceral integration
PTSD & Sound Therapy

Acoustic intervention
for trauma processing

A Cochrane Review (Bradt & Dileo, 2010) across 26 studies found music therapy produced significant reductions in PTSD symptom severity, anxiety, and depression. Sound-based interventions access somatic memory via pre-verbal pathways bypassed by standard cognitive approaches.

Bradt & Dileo 2010Cochrane ReviewPTSD
Language & Integration

Semantic change as
outcome measure

Pennebaker's LIWC framework (decades of research) shows that linguistic shifts — from past to present tense, from avoidance to embodiment — are reliable predictors of therapeutic integration. Awe Field's NLP layer tracks this evolution across the programme.

Pennebaker LIWCLinguistic analysisIntegration tracking
Awe Field is a synthesis of established clinical science — not a new-age experiment, but a structured application of peer-reviewed mechanisms.

Music & Sound Design

A curated and adaptive
sonic architecture

The Awe Field protocol draws from multiple musical and acoustic approaches — each selected based on its capacity to guide shifts in perception, emotion, and physiological state.

The sonic approaches

Ambient & Minimal Compositions

For nervous system regulation and grounding — a stable acoustic container from which deeper states become accessible.

Psychoacoustic Textures

To influence spatial perception and internal focus. Sound that operates below the level of conscious attention.

Rhythmic Entrainment

To support state transitions and emotional processing. Tempo and rhythm as physiological tools calibrated to the specific phase of the journey.

Harmonic & Tonal Structures

Designed to evoke tension, release, and resolution — moving emotional material without requiring narrative.

Dynamic Soundscapes

That evolve in response to the listener's physiological state. Sound as the primary guide through different depths of experience.

Spatial & Immersive Sound

Drawing on electroacoustic composition and diffusion. The spatial dimension of sound as a therapeutic tool.
